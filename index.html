Why Most Engineers Get Overlooked

Most young engineers think getting hired comes down to how much they know.

It doesn’t.

I’ve sat in on a lot of interviews, and I’ve seen the same thing happen repeatedly.

Very capable engineers talk themselves out of the job.

Not because they lack skill. Not because they didn’t work hard enough.

Because they can’t clearly communicate what they know.

They struggle to explain their experience, tell a compelling story, and present themselves with confidence.

In competitive environments like aerospace, that’s everything.

Students spend years grinding through engineering school just trying to survive, but along the way they miss how important positioning and communication really are.

That’s at least half the battle.

Why I Built This Bootcamp

My name is Davis Booth.

I worked at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center on the Roman Space Telescope, alongside some of the most talented engineers in the world.

I also worked at United Launch Alliance, where I sat console during the Atlas rocket launch that sent Suni Williams and Butch Wilmore to the International Space Station.

I helped certify the maiden launch of the Vulcan rocket, which will carry ULA into the next decade and replace both the Delta and Atlas platforms.

I’ve been fortunate enough to experience the kind of engineering work most people only dream about.

Now I want to help make that a reality for as many engineers as possible.

And it starts with taking ownership of your future and being honest about where you are today.
