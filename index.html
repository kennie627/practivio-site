Why Most Engineers Get Overlooked

Most young engineers think getting the job comes down to how much they know.

It doesn’t.

I’ve sat in on a lot of interviews.

And I’ve seen something happen over and over again… very capable engineers talk themselves out of the job.

Not because they lack skill. Not because they didn’t study hard enough.

Because they couldn’t communicate what they know.

They couldn’t clearly explain their experience. They couldn’t tell a compelling story. They couldn’t present themselves with confidence.

And in competitive environments like aerospace, that’s everything.

Students spend years grinding through engineering school just trying to survive.

But along the way, they miss one of the most important parts of the process: how to position themselves, how to communicate their value, and how to stand out in a room full of other smart engineers.

That’s at least half the battle. Probably more.
